The 2020 Tokyo Olympics opening ceremony went ahead with few spectators. Protesters outside of the Olympic Stadium continued to call for the Games to be cancelled. Watch the video to understand all the fuss. ?? ?? ?? The 2020 Tokyo Olympics have begun. When it was announced in 2013 that Tokyo had won its bid for the 2020 Olympics, the atmosphere in Japan was one of excitement. People cheered and celebrated what was seen to be an excellent showcase opportunity for the country. But after being delayed for a year due to the pandemic that is still ongoing, the feeling in the host country has shifted. Many in Japan have expressed concerns about the Olympics and some don’t want the Games to be held this summer at all. The Japanese flag enters Olympic Stadium. One flagbearer, in grey, was a health-care first responder. (Image credit: Antonin Thuillier/AFP/Getty Images) These fears and criticisms hung over the opening ceremony as the world watched to see how Japan would handle such a large international event while cases of COVID-19 surge in their country. Controlling COVID-19 in Tokyo As of Friday, 110 Olympic-accredited people in Japan, including 13 athletes, have tested positive for COVID-19 since July 1. The stands during the opening ceremony were virtually empty with no public spectators in attendance. (Image credit: Leon Neal/The Associated Press) Tokyo reported 1,359 cases of COVID-19 on Thursday. The highest case count since January. Since July 12, the host city has been under a state of emergency that won’t end until at least August 22. WHO president backs the Games The director of the World Health Organization (WHO), Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus, who has overseen the international response to the pandemic, gave his support to the Games. On Wednesday he thanked both the government of Japan and the International Olympic Committee (IOC) for their efforts. "May the Olympic torch be a symbol of hope that traverses the planet," said Tedros. Medal count The first medals of the Tokyo Olympics will be won in Shooting (women’s 10m air rifle) on July 24 at 11:25 a.m. Japan time (July 23 at 10:25 p.m. ET). Opening ceremony in pictures Performers dance in front of wooden Olympic rings during the opening ceremony at the Olympic Stadium in Tokyo. The wood for the rings came from all over the world, including Canada. Most did not participate in the parade. Some athletes hadn't arrived, while others focussed on preparing for their events. (Image credit: Clive Brunskill/Getty Images) Team Japan tennis player, Naomi Osaka lit the Olympic torch. Osaka is ranked number one in the world in women’s tennis. She is returning to competition for the first time in nearly two months after she withdrew from the French Open to take a mental health break.
